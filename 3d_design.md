# **3-d Designing** 
## What is 3d-design?
The process of using computer software to create objects in three dimensions, length, breadth, and height, is known as 3D design. 3D designs appear realistic and can be viewed from multiple perspectives, in contrast with 2D drawings. These designs can be applied to 3D printing, games, engineering, animation, and architecture.

A designer builds models of tools, structures, machinery, and other creative products by combining shapes.

# CAD (Computer-Aided Design)
A computer-aided design is what CAD stands for. It is the creation, modification, and development of 2D and 3D designs using computer software. CAD enables engineers and designers in producing precise digital models before they start producing tangible products.
<img  src="https://drive.google.com/thumbnail?id=1jCHDxn7L6P49GtJuYIDK4wc0CH_I1tip&sz=w800" alt="Profile Photo">*source:google*

It is frequently used to design buildings, machinery, parts, and other items that can be 3D printed or constructed later.

**In CAD, every object is created using dimensions such as:**

1) Length

2) Width

3) Height

4) Angles and measurements

## **Why CAD is Important**

# Subtractive and Additive Manufacturing
## Introduction
Additive manufacturing and subtractive manufacturing are the two primary techniques used in digital fabrication and 3D design to manufacture products. These techniques explain how materials are employed to create finished goods.
<img  src="https://drive.google.com/thumbnail?id=1abC0522QYMlW7-g_MOtyikTGyzYeucjy&sz=w800" alt="Profile Photo">*source google*
## 1. Additive Manufacturing
The process of creating an object by layering on material is known as additive manufacturing.

 **How it Works**

 A machine adds material step-by-step until the full object is formed.

 Example: *3D printing*
<img  src="https://drive.google.com/thumbnail?id=1BLae4-dHC0cuHFlDU-nvQLi2R47kwgXo&sz=w800" alt="Profile Photo">*source google*

## 2. Subtractive Manufacturing
The process of making an object by taking material out of a solid block is known as subtractive manufacturing.

**How it works**

The final shape is created by cutting, drilling, or carving away material.

**example:**

1) CNC milling machine
<img  src="https://drive.google.com/thumbnail?id=1tI8cBYbILZuB_Rni9N2HZi-WdBgC1ppM&sz=w800" alt="Profile Photo">

2) Laser cutting
<img  src="https://drive.google.com/thumbnail?id=1snDIGQ7cSaz1YBlwnCRB74g3QFAAj3L0&sz=w800" alt="Profile Photo">

| Additive Manufacturing   | Subtractive Manufacturing         |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------- |
| Material is added        | Material is removed               |
| Less waste               | More waste produced               |
| Used in 3D printing      | Used in CNC and laser cutting     |
| Good for complex designs | Good for precise mechanical parts |

# Parametric Design
## What is Parametric Design?
Every component of the model in parametric design depends on particular values, such as height, width, length, or angles.
A single measurement change instantly changes the entire design.
## How Parametric Design Works

1) Create a sketch.

2) Add dimensions and constraints.

3) Convert the sketch into a 3D model.

4) Change parameters when needed.

5) The design updates automatically.

## What is Kerf?
When cutting a piece, a laser or other cutting instrument removes a tiny quantity of material called a kerf. It's crucial to take the kerf into account when designing parts because failing to do so could result in improper fit.

## STL, OBJ, and G-code

In 3D design, digital files store the information needed to create a model:

* STL – Stores the 3D shape of a model.

* OBJ – Also stores the 3D shape and can include color and texture information.

* G-code – Contains instructions for the 3D printer, telling it how to move and build the model layer by layer.
