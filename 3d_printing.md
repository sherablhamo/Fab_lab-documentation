Slicing and Generating G-code:

* Quality / Speed: Choose between better print quality or faster printing. Higher quality takes more time.

* Print Settings: Adjust things like layer height, wall thickness, and temperature to get good results.

* Material Selection: Pick the right filament (like PLA or ABS) depending on what you are making and your printer.

* Printer Selection: Select the correct printer so the settings match your machine.

* Infill: Decide how solid the inside of the print should be, which affects strength and material use.

### Fill Density.
Most 3D prints work well with an infill of around 10–15%. In some cases, if the top layers of the model close smoothly, it is possible to print with 0% infill (hollow), but this is generally not recommended. If you want the object to be stronger, more rigid, or heavier, you can increase the infill percentage. However, going above 30% is usually unnecessary for most prints. You can also set the infill to 100%, which makes the object completely solid and uses a rectilinear pattern. Keep in mind that using full infill may reduce the visual quality of the final print. 

* **Why the Default Infill in PrusaSlicer is 15%**
In PrusaSlicer, the standard infill is set to 15%, which represents the internal pattern inside a 3D printed object.

 This value is selected because it offers several practical benefits:

Strength vs Material Efficiency: A 15% infill creates a solid enough structure for most uses while avoiding unnecessary use of filament.

Faster Printing: Using a lower infill reduces both the material needed and the total printing time, making the process more efficient.

Beginner-Friendly Base Setting: This percentage works well as a general starting point, allowing new users to print successfully without adjustments, while still giving flexibility for advanced users to modify it as needed.

### Making adjustments during a print
The printing process began at a reduced speed of 20% of the machine’s maximum capacity to maintain high-quality output, especially for the model’s curved structure. Once the print demonstrated good stability, the speed was carefully increased to 30%. This gradual adjustment helped achieve an effective balance between maintaining print quality and reducing overall printing time, resulting in a clean and precise final product.
